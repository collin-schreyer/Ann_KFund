# Example Compliance Questions

These are typical questions State Department personnel need to answer when reviewing export scenarios.

## Query 1: Dual-Use Technology Classification

**Question:** 
"We have a request to export high-performance GPS receivers to a commercial shipping company in Country X. The receivers have accuracy within 1 meter and can operate at altitudes up to 60,000 feet. Are these subject to ITAR or EAR? What license is required?"

**Key Considerations:**
- Altitude and accuracy specifications
- End-user type (commercial vs. military)
- Potential military applications
- Country X's relationship with U.S.

---

## Query 2: Technical Data Release

**Question:**
"Our company wants to hire a foreign national engineer from Country Y to work on a project involving design specifications for aircraft navigation systems. The systems are used in both commercial and military aircraft. Do we need a license for this 'deemed export'?"

**Key Considerations:**
- Citizenship of foreign national
- Whether technical data is ITAR-controlled
- Whether data is in public domain
- Type of access required

---

## Query 3: Re-export Scenario

**Question:**
"A Canadian company purchased U.S.-origin thermal imaging cameras under the Canadian exemption. Now they want to integrate them into a system for export to Country Z. What approvals are needed?"

**Key Considerations:**
- Canadian exemption limitations
- Re-export requirements
- End-use in Country Z
- U.S. content percentage

---

## Query 4: Public Domain Determination

**Question:**
"We presented technical specifications for our defense communication system at an international conference. The presentation is now available on the conference website. Can we share this information with foreign customers without a license?"

**Key Considerations:**
- Public domain definition under ITAR
- Whether information was properly released
- Scope of information disclosed
- Additional technical data beyond presentation

---

## Query 5: End-Use Certificate

**Question:**
"We're exporting night vision equipment to the police force of Country A (a NATO ally). Do we need an end-use certificate? What documentation is required?"

**Key Considerations:**
- Whether item is Significant Military Equipment (SME)
- NATO ally status
- End-user type (police vs. military)
- Specific USML category requirements

---

## Query 6: De Minimis Calculation

**Question:**
"Our German subsidiary manufactures industrial robots that contain 15% U.S.-origin controlled components by value. They want to export these robots to Country B. Do they need U.S. authorization?"

**Key Considerations:**
- De minimis threshold for Country B
- Whether Country B is embargoed
- Type of controlled components
- ECCN classification of U.S. content

---

## Query 7: Temporary Export

**Question:**
"We need to send a prototype weapons targeting system to the UK for testing at a government facility for 90 days, then it will return to the U.S. What type of authorization do we need?"

**Key Considerations:**
- Temporary export provisions
- UK as NATO ally
- Government end-user
- Return timeline

---

## Query 8: Encryption Software

**Question:**
"Our software includes 256-bit AES encryption and will be sold commercially worldwide through our website. Is this subject to ITAR or EAR? Can we use the published encryption exemption?"

**Key Considerations:**
- Encryption strength
- Commercial vs. military application
- Public availability
- Self-classification requirements

---

## Query 9: Training Services

**Question:**
"We're providing maintenance training to foreign military personnel on U.S.-origin helicopters at our facility in Texas. The training covers engine repair and avionics troubleshooting. Do we need a license?"

**Key Considerations:**
- Defense services definition
- Training on USML items
- Location of training (U.S. vs. abroad)
- Nationality of trainees

---

## Query 10: Spare Parts Export

**Question:**
"Country C purchased fighter aircraft from us 10 years ago under a government-to-government FMS sale. They now need spare parts for routine maintenance. What's the process for exporting these parts?"

**Key Considerations:**
- FMS vs. DCS procedures
- Original sale authorization
- Current relationship with Country C
- Type of spare parts (consumables vs. major components)
