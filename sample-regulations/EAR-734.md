# Export Administration Regulations (EAR) - Part 734

## § 734.1 Scope

The Export Administration Regulations (EAR) regulate the export and reexport of commercial and dual-use items.

## § 734.2 Types of Items Subject to the EAR

(a) All items in the United States, including in a U.S. Foreign Trade Zone or moving in transit through the United States from one foreign country to another.

(b) All U.S. origin items wherever located.

(c) Certain foreign-made items that incorporate controlled U.S.-origin items or are based on certain U.S. technology or software.

## § 734.3 Items Subject to the EAR

### (a) Items Not Subject to the EAR

The following items are NOT subject to the EAR:

(1) Items subject to the export control jurisdiction of the Department of State (ITAR)
(2) Items subject to the jurisdiction of the Nuclear Regulatory Commission
(3) Items subject to the jurisdiction of the Department of Energy

### (b) Dual-Use Items

Items that have both commercial and military applications, including:

- Certain telecommunications equipment
- Encryption technology
- High-performance computers
- Advanced materials and composites
- Navigation and avionics equipment

## § 734.4 De Minimis Rules

### (a) U.S. Content Thresholds

Reexports of foreign-made items containing U.S.-origin controlled content:

(1) 25% threshold for most countries
(2) 10% threshold for embargoed destinations (Country Group E:1)
(3) 0% for certain encryption items

### (b) Calculation Method

Calculate the percentage of controlled U.S. content by dividing:
- The value of controlled U.S.-origin items
- By the total value of the foreign-made product

## § 734.7 Published Encryption

Encryption software that is publicly available is not subject to the EAR when:

(a) It is published or will be published
(b) It arises during, or results from, fundamental research
(c) It is educational information

## § 734.9 Foreign Availability

Items may be eligible for export license exception if:

(a) Comparable items are available from non-U.S. sources without effective restrictions
(b) Foreign availability has been established through BIS procedures
(c) The item meets specific criteria for the license exception
