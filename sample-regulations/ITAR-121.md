# International Traffic in Arms Regulations (ITAR) - Part 121

## ยง 121.1 General

The United States Munitions List (USML) identifies defense articles and defense services subject to the export control authorities of the Department of State.

## ยง 121.8 Significant Military Equipment (SME)

Significant military equipment (SME) means articles for which special export controls are warranted because of their capacity for substantial military utility or capability.

### Category I - Firearms, Close Assault Weapons and Combat Shotguns

(a) Nonautomatic and semi-automatic firearms to caliber .50 inclusive (12.7 mm).

(b) Fully automatic firearms to .50 caliber inclusive (12.7 mm).

(c) Firearms or other weapons (e.g., insurgency-counterinsurgency, close assault weapons systems) having a special military application regardless of caliber.

### Category IV - Launch Vehicles, Guided Missiles, Ballistic Missiles, Rockets, Torpedoes, Bombs, and Mines

(a) Rockets, space launch vehicles (SLVs), missiles, bombs, torpedoes, depth charges, mines, and grenades, as follows:

(1) Rockets, SLVs, and missiles capable of delivering at least a 500 kg payload to a range of at least 300 km.

(2) Anti-tank grenades and rockets with an explosive charge greater than 0.5 kg.

## ยง 121.16 Exemptions

(a) The following exemptions apply to items on the USML:

(1) Information in the public domain
(2) Educational information
(3) Basic marketing information

### Public Domain Definition

Technical data that is published and generally accessible or available to the public through:
- Sales at newsstands and bookstores
- Subscriptions available without restriction
- Libraries open to the public
- Patents available at patent offices
- Public release at conferences, meetings, or seminars

## ยง 121.10 End-Use Certificate Requirements

Certain exports require end-use certificates from the importing country's government, certifying:

(a) The defense article will not be transferred to unauthorized parties
(b) The article will be used only for stated purposes
(c) The importing government accepts responsibility for compliance
