# K Fund (EDCS) - Emergencies in the Diplomatic and Consular Service

## 22 U.S.C. § 2671 - Emergency Expenditures

### (a) Authorization
The Secretary of State may use funds appropriated for Emergencies in the Diplomatic and Consular Service (EDCS), commonly known as the "K Fund," for:

1. **Representation expenses** - Expenses of a representational character including:
   - Official entertainment of foreign dignitaries
   - Gifts to foreign officials and dignitaries
   - Expenses incurred in extending courtesies to foreign visitors
   - Representational activities that advance U.S. foreign policy interests

2. **Emergency expenses** - Unforeseen emergencies arising in the diplomatic and consular service

### (b) Limitations
- Expenditures must serve a legitimate foreign affairs purpose
- Gifts must be appropriate to the occasion and recipient's position
- Entertainment expenses must be reasonable and customary

---

## Allowable K Fund Expenditures

### Category 1: Gifts to Foreign Officials
**Statutory Authority:** 22 U.S.C. § 2694

Allowable items include:
- Commemorative items bearing official seals
- American-made artisan products
- Books and publications about American history/culture
- Crystal, silver, or other presentation items
- Items reflecting the recipient's interests or position

**Requirements:**
- Gift value must be appropriate to recipient's rank
- Must be presented in official capacity
- Documentation of recipient and occasion required

### Category 2: Official Entertainment
**Statutory Authority:** 22 U.S.C. § 2671(a)

Allowable expenses include:
- Meals and refreshments for foreign officials
- Venue costs for representational events
- Catering for diplomatic functions
- Floral arrangements for official events
- Printed materials (menus, programs, place cards)

**Requirements:**
- Event must have foreign affairs purpose
- Guest list must include foreign officials or dignitaries
- Secretary or designee must approve

### Category 3: Representational Activities
**Statutory Authority:** 22 U.S.C. § 2671(a)

Allowable activities include:
- Cultural events honoring foreign visitors
- Receptions for diplomatic delegations
- Ceremonies involving foreign participation
- Protocol courtesies extended to foreign dignitaries

---

## Non-Allowable K Fund Expenditures

The following are NOT allowable under K Fund:

1. **Purely domestic events** - Events without foreign affairs nexus
2. **Personal expenses** - Items for personal use by U.S. officials
3. **Operational costs** - Infrastructure, equipment, or logistics that don't directly serve representational purposes
4. **Gifts to U.S. persons** - K Fund cannot be used for gifts to American citizens
5. **Routine office expenses** - Supplies, equipment, or services for normal operations

---

## Event Classification Rules

### Rule 1: Foreign Affairs Nexus
Every K Fund expenditure must have a clear connection to foreign affairs. Ask:
- Does this expense involve foreign officials or dignitaries?
- Does this advance U.S. diplomatic interests?
- Is this a representational activity?

### Rule 2: Recipient Test
For hospitality expenses, determine who receives the benefit:
- **Foreign officials receive benefit** → K Fund allowable
- **Only U.S. officials receive benefit** → NOT K Fund allowable
- **Mixed benefit** → Prorate based on foreign participation

### Rule 3: Gift Appropriateness
Gifts must be:
- Appropriate to the recipient's rank and position
- Suitable for the occasion
- Not extravagant or excessive
- Properly documented

### Rule 4: Entertainment Reasonableness
Entertainment expenses must be:
- Customary for the type of event
- Reasonable in amount
- Properly authorized
- Documented with guest lists and purpose

---

## Documentation Requirements

All K Fund expenditures require:

1. **Purpose Statement** - Clear explanation of foreign affairs purpose
2. **Guest List** - Names and titles of foreign officials present
3. **Authorization** - Approval from appropriate authority
4. **Receipts** - Itemized receipts for all expenses
5. **Event Description** - Summary of the event and its diplomatic significance

---

## Approval Authorities

| Expenditure Amount | Approval Required |
|-------------------|-------------------|
| Under $5,000 | Bureau Executive Director |
| $5,000 - $25,000 | Assistant Secretary |
| $25,000 - $100,000 | Under Secretary |
| Over $100,000 | Deputy Secretary or Secretary |

---

## Audit and Compliance

K Fund expenditures are subject to:
- Inspector General review
- GAO audit authority
- Congressional oversight
- Annual reporting requirements

Certifying officers are protected under 31 U.S.C. § 3528 when relying on legal determinations from the Office of the Legal Adviser.
