K Fund Allocation for State Department Events

Purpose: This tool helps certifying officers and budget staff determine which event expenses are allowable under the K Fund (EDCS - Emergencies in the Diplomatic and Consular Service) appropriation per 22 U.S.C. ¬ß 2671 and related authorities.

What is the K Fund?

The K Fund, formally known as Emergencies in the Diplomatic and Consular Service (EDCS), is a State Department appropriation authorized under 22 U.S.C. ¬ß 2671. It funds representational expenses including official entertainment of foreign dignitaries, gifts to foreign officials, and courtesies extended to foreign visitors in support of U.S. foreign policy objectives.

The Challenge

Certifying officers must determine which event line items are allowable under K Fund versus other appropriations. This requires analyzing each expense against complex legal authorities including 22 U.S.C. ¬ß 2671, 22 U.S.C. ¬ß 2694, and GAO guidance. Manual classification is time-consuming and risks appropriations violations.

Our AI Solution

This tool automatically classifies event line items by applying K Fund allowability rules:

  • Identifies expenses that are always K Fund allowable (gifts to foreign officials, hospitality)
  • Flags expenses that are never allowable (security, transportation, capital costs)
  • Routes unclear items to Legal Adviser for determination
  • Calculates proration for mixed foreign/domestic events
  • Provides legal citations for every classification
